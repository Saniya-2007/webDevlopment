Welcome to Hyderabad

Hyderabad is the capital of Telangana and one of India's major metropolitan cities, known for its rich history, unique blend of cultures, and significant contributions to information technology, biotechnology, and pharmaceuticals. Founded in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah around the Charminar, the city has evolved as a trading, cultural, and architectural center, especially under the rule of the Nizams. Hyderabad is famous for its historic sites such as the Charminar, Golconda Fort, and Qutb Shahi Tombs, as well as its vibrant bazaars like Laad Bazaar, known for bangles and pearls. The city is also renowned for its culinary delights, particularly the Hyderabadi Biryani. In recent decades, Hyderabad has emerged as a major IT hub, often referred to as "Cyberabad," hosting numerous multinational corporations and tech parks. The city's diverse population and rich cultural tapestry make it a unique blend of tradition and modernity.

Hyderabad, the City of Pearls, is a vibrant metropolis that beautifully blends its rich historical heritage with modern advancements. Known for its iconic Charminar, bustling bazaars, and delectable cuisine, Hyderabad offers a unique cultural experience. The city is also a major IT hub, attracting professionals from around the world. With its warm hospitality and diverse attractions, Hyderabad is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking both tradition and innovation.Situated on the Musi River on the Telangana Plateau, Hyderabad occupies about 650 square kilometers and has a population exceeding 6.8 million (2011 census), making it India’s fourth-most populous city and a major economic hub. Its warm, monsoonal climate features most rainfall between June and October Hyderabad is sometimes called the "City of Pearls" due to its historical diamond and pearl trading. The city is multilingual, with significant communities speaking Telugu, Urdu (noted for its distinctive Hyderabadi dialect), Hindi, and English. Cosmopolitan in outlook, Hyderabad’s architecture, cuisine, and diverse traditions reflect a blend of indigenous, Islamic, and colonial influences.This legacy gives the city a distinctive identity celebrated through architecture, art, food, and festivities.
