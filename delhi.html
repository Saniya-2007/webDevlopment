Welcome to Delhi

Delhi, the capital city of India, is a vibrant metropolis that seamlessly blends history with modernity. Known for its rich cultural heritage, Delhi boasts iconic landmarks such as the Red Fort, Qutub Minar, and India Gate. The city is a melting pot of diverse cultures, languages, and cuisines, offering a unique experience to visitors. From bustling markets like Chandni Chowk to serene gardens like Lodhi Gardens, Delhi has something for everyone. The city's dynamic atmosphere is further enhanced by its thriving arts scene, historical museums, and lively festivals. Whether you're exploring ancient monuments or indulging in contemporary culinary delights, Delhi promises an unforgettable journey through time and culture. The earliest mention of a settlement in the region appears in the epic Mahabharata, which describes Indraprastha—considered the first city of Delhi—as the seat of the Pandavas around 1400 BCE. Throughout history, the region has been shaped by the rise and fall of multiple dynasties, from the Tomaras (who established Lal Kot in the 11th century) and Chauhans (who expanded the city into Qila Rai Pithora), to the Sultanate era marked by the construction of iconic structures like the Quṭb Mīnār. The sequence of city-building continued through the establishment of Siri, Tughlaqabad, and Shahjahanabad (the walled Old City built by Shah Jahan in the 17th century), which remains the historic heart of Delhi today.

Delhi’s cultural heritage is showcased through its iconic monuments such as the Red Fort, Humayun’s Tomb, India Gate, Jama Masjid alongside institutions like the National Museum and India Habitat Centre. The city’s architecture reflects a unique blend of Persian, Turkic and contemporary Indian styles. Festivals, music, dance, literature, and visual arts find vibrant expression cultural events such as the Delhi International Arts Festival, and traditional crafts ranging from intricate jewelry to handwoven textiles.Despite historic challenges, Delhi is a city of resilience. It faces issues common to megacities—rapid urbanization, environmental stress, traffic congestion social disparities—yet continues to innovate with sustainable urban planning and civic reforms. Its diverse population is knitted together by a shared identity grounded in history.
